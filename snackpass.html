Snackpass Redesign
Type: UI/UX, User Interview, Mobile
Tools: Figma, Procreate
Date: February 2021
Snackpass is a mobile app that allows users to order their food ahead of time for either pickup or delivery. What makes Snackpass different from other food-ordering apps is that it feels like a social media app. When users place an order on Snackpass, they receive a reward point and can then send a reward point to a friend. By collecting enough reward points, users can earn deals from different restaurants. Through a friend feed, users can also see what their friends are ordering. Targeting college campuses across the United States, Snackpass can already be seen on 11 campuses. As someone who uses Snackpass on a regular basis when studying at Brown University, I noticed some limitations of the mobile app and decided to redesign it.
Current Design:
User Interviews:
Goal: Interview 5 Brown University students to gain insight on their experience of using Snackpass.
Interview Questions:
  1. How often do you use Snackpass?
  2. Please walk me through your last Snackpass order.
  3. On a scale of 1-10, rate the level of difficulty in using the app. In this scale, 1 means “not difficult at all” and 10 means “very difficult.”
  4. How do you feel about the social aspect of the app?
  5. What is 1 feature you like the most about Snackpass?
  6. What is 1 feature you dislike the most about Snackpass?
Interview Responses:
Goals:
After reading the responses to the interview questions, I brainstormed 3 goals.
  1. The meal page should organize restaurant options clearly so that they are easy to navigate.
  2. The meal page should include other filter options beyond the distances of the restaurants and their discounts.
  3. There should be a personalized profile page that allows users to quickly reorder their past order and to see their reward points.

Concluding Thoughts:
This project taught me the importance of accessibility and memorability. What design choices allow users to quickly access information and encourage them to use the app again? Although I believe that this redesign improves navigation, there are some changes that can still be made. For example, from the user interview, I learned that it is difficult to receive a refund if a restaurant messes up its transaction. According to the Snackpass website, the user has to directly call the restaurant and request a refund. It would be useful if there is a feature that allows users to request a refund without having to call the restaurant and wait for a response. This is something I am thinking about for a future project.
