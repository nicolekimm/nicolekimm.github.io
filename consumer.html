Consumer Psychology Research
Type: Research
Tools: R, Qualtrics, Amazon Mechanical Turk
Date: June-August 2019

During my freshman year at Brown, I took a psychology class called Making Decisions. Throughout the semester, I learned about the psychology of human decision-making and how it impacts both individual action and behavior as well as social policy. I became fascinated by how consumers make decisions in their daily lives and wanted to learn more about consumer psychology research. So, that summer, I collaborated with Professor Uma Karmarkar from the Rady School of Management at UCSD and designed a study to research how the familiarity of display items impacts display-set effects in consumer decision-making.
